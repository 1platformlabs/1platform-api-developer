# Releases & updates

Your Atlas App gets two kinds of updates: instant over-the-air updates for most changes, and full store releases for changes to the app's native identity. Each tenant has its **own update channel**, so updates target only your audience.

## Two ways the app updates

- **Over-the-air (OTA)** — most improvements (screens, fixes, copy) can be delivered without a store submission. They're governed per tenant by your update channel, so an update reaches only your build.
- **Store release** — changes to the app's **native identity** (name, icon, splash, bundle id) or to platform requirements need a new build submitted to the App Store and Google Play.

Many things never need any release at all: your colors, languages, feature flags, catalog, plans and curated rails are all read at runtime from your tenant configuration and update instantly.

## Build channels

Each tenant build is tied to its own release channel (for example, separate development, preview and production channels). This keeps updates isolated: rolling out a change to one tenant doesn't touch another, and a tenant can run its own version independently.

## What you control

- **When to publish** — you decide when an update goes to your audience.
- **Per-tenant isolation** — because channels are per tenant, a tenant sold or handed off to a new owner can be operated on its own release cadence.

Day-to-day operational steps (building, submitting and channel setup) are handled as operator tasks outside the app itself.
