# Invoicing businesses

Before you can issue invoices you need at least one **issuing business** — the entity your invoices are sent from. The **Businesses** screen (reached via *New invoice* or the Invoicing area) is where you manage them.

## What you see

A table lists each business with its name, tax ID, regime, contact email, and an *Active* status badge. Selecting a business name opens its detail view. If you have no businesses yet, an empty state invites you to configure your first issuer.

## Adding a business

1. Select **Add business**.
2. Fill in the form:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Business name** | Required. The issuer's legal name. |
| **Tax ID** | Required. The issuer's tax identifier. |
| **Address** | Optional. |
| **Branch name** | Required. A first branch is created and linked to your account. |
| **Commerce ID** | Required. Identifies the branch. |

3. Select **Save business**. The new issuer appears in the table.

When you create a business, a first branch is set up for you automatically, so you're ready to invoice immediately.

## Adding more branches

A business can have several branches. On any row, choose **Add branch**, enter the branch name and commerce ID, and save. A confirmation message tells you the branch was added.

## Why it matters

When you issue an invoice you pick which business (and branch) it comes from. Keeping this list accurate ensures every invoice is attributed to the right issuer. Return to [Invoicing](/docs/products/dashboard/invoicing) to issue and track invoices once a business exists.
