# App Store presence

Your branded Atlas App is published as its own app on the **App Store** and **Google Play**, under your own store accounts. To your audience, it's simply *your* app — there's no sign of a shared platform.

## What your audience sees

- **A standalone listing** — your app name, icon, screenshots and description on each store.
- **Your identity** — the app's unique id (bundle / package id) is derived from your brand, so the listing is unmistakably yours.
- **Deep links** — store links to your app can be surfaced from your website and within your tenant configuration, so visitors can jump straight to install.

## What you provide

- **Store metadata** — app name, description, screenshots and category for each store listing.
- **Store accounts** — the published app is owned by *your* App Store and Google Play accounts. This is what makes a tenant fully transferable: ownership of the listing moves with the app.
- **Icon & artwork** — your icon and splash are part of the build (see [Getting started](/docs/products/atlas-app/getting-started)).

## Ownership & transfer

Because the listing lives in your store accounts and the app's identity is your own, a tenant's app can be transferred to a new owner without affecting any other tenant. The new owner takes over the store listing with the same app id and continues to operate it independently.
