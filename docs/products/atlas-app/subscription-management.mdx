# Subscription management

The Atlas App follows a **reader-app model**: members can *view* their membership inside the app, but they *manage* it on the web. There are no native in-app purchases, which keeps a single source of truth for billing and respects store rules.

## What your audience gets

- **Subscription screen** — shows the member's current plan with three clear states:
  - **No plan** — "You don't have an active plan" with an upgrade prompt.
  - **Active** — the current plan and next renewal date.
  - **Expired** — "Your plan expired" with a renew prompt.
- **Manage on the web** — every management action (subscribe, change plan, renew) opens your tenant's website to complete the change. The app never charges through the store.
- **Payment history** — members can review their past payments inside the app.
- **Plan catalog** — a membership screen presents the available plans (for example a free tier and paid tiers), marking the member's current plan.
- **Offline-friendly** — if the device is offline, the app shows the last known subscription from its cache.

## What you control

- **Plans & pricing** — defined for your tenant and surfaced in the app; no prices are hardcoded.
- **Web management pages** — the app links to your tenant's web pages to choose a plan and manage a subscription.
- **Access enforcement** — whether membership is required to use the app at all is the subscription gate covered in [Freemium](/docs/products/atlas-app/freemium).

Once a member completes a change on the web and returns to the app, their new status is honored automatically.
