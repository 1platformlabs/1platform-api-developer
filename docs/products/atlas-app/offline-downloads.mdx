# Offline downloads

Reliable offline access is a flagship behaviour of the Atlas App. Readers can download titles and use them with no connection, and a dedicated manager keeps every download visible and recoverable.

## What your audience gets

- **Download manager** — a screen listing every downloaded or queued title with its status (downloaded, downloading with a progress bar, queued, paused, or error). Readers can pause, resume or remove any item, and pull to refresh.
- **Quota indicator** — a header shows how many titles are downloaded and how much of the limit is used.
- **Trustworthy downloads** — a download is only treated as complete when the file is fully written, guarding against truncated transfers. If a download's access window expires, the app quietly re-requests it before resuming — no silent failures.
- **Offline reading & playback** — downloaded titles open entirely offline, with an offline banner so the reader knows they're not connected.

## Quotas: free vs. member

- **Free tier** — a capped number of downloads per day.
- **Member plan** — unlimited downloads.

The server is the source of truth for what each member is entitled to; the app simply reflects the current limit and prevents attempts beyond it.

## What you control

- **Downloads** — a per-tenant feature flag turns offline downloads on or off for your whole audience.
- Membership plans (managed on the web) determine whether a member is on the capped free tier or unlimited. See [Subscription management](/docs/products/atlas-app/subscription-management) and [Freemium](/docs/products/atlas-app/freemium).
