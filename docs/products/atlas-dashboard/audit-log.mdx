# Audit Log

The **Audit Log** screen (`/admin/audit-log`) records significant actions taken in your dashboard, so
you can see who changed what and when. It's useful for accountability across your team and for
troubleshooting when something looks different from what you expected.

## What it shows

The log is a business-level history of meaningful events — for example content being published or
archived, settings updated, plans changed, or members invited. Each entry is written in plain language
rather than raw technical detail, so you can scan it without a developer.

A typical entry tells you:

- **Who** performed the action (the operator's name).
- **What** changed (the action and the affected item or setting).
- **When** it happened.

## How to use it

- **Verify a change** — confirm that a publish, price change, or settings update actually went through.
- **Coordinate a team** — see what a colleague did before you make your own edit.
- **Troubleshoot** — if the storefront changed unexpectedly, the log helps you trace the cause.
