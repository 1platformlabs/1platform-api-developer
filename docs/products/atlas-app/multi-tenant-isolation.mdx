# Multi-tenant isolation

Every Atlas App is isolated at three levels. The result: operating, transferring or selling one tenant never affects any other, even though they share a codebase.

## Three levels of isolation

| Level | What's isolated |
|---|---|
| **Data** | All member data — accounts, entitlements, reading progress, watchlist, devices, push registrations and content — is scoped to your tenant. No reader ever sees another tenant's data. |
| **Configuration & branding** | Your theme, languages, feature flags and store links live in your tenant configuration, editable by your admin without a new build. One tenant's settings are never readable or writable by another. |
| **App build** | Your brand profile (name, icon, splash, scheme, bundle id) and your own release channel belong only to your build. Changing your brand never affects another tenant's app. |

An installed app only ever knows its own tenant — it has no way to see or reach any other tenant.

## Why it matters for operators

This isolation is what makes a branded app a true asset you can own and transfer:

- **Independent operation** — your audience, content and updates are yours alone.
- **Safe handoff or sale** — a tenant can be transferred to a new owner by moving the store listing, the tenant ownership, and the brand profile. The new owner runs it independently, and no other tenant is touched.
- **Privacy & trust** — because data never crosses tenants, each reader's library and account stay private to your tenant.

## What you control

Your admin manages your configuration and branding directly; the per-tenant data scoping and build isolation are guaranteed by the platform automatically.
