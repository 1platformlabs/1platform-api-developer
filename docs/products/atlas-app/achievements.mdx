# Achievements

The Atlas App can reward and motivate your audience with achievements — medals readers unlock as they engage with your library.

## What your audience gets

- **Achievements grid** — a screen showing "N of M unlocked" above a three-column grid of medals. Each earned medal shows its emblem, name, and the date it was earned.
- **Locked vs. unlocked** — medals the reader hasn't earned yet are shown dimmed, so there's always something to aim for.
- **Clear states** — the screen handles loading, an empty catalog, errors with a retry, and an offline view.

Achievements give readers a sense of progress and a reason to come back — a light, optional layer of engagement on top of the core reading experience.

## What you control

- **Achievement catalog** — the set of available medals is defined per tenant in your configuration, so you decide which achievements your audience can earn and how they're named.
- Achievements are scoped to each reader within your tenant; a reader only ever sees their own progress.

If you don't define an achievement catalog, the screen simply shows its empty state — the feature is opt-in by configuration.
