# Reader experience

The reader is the heart of a library-style Atlas App. When a reader opens a title, the app restores their saved position, mounts the right reader for the format, and keeps their place safe even offline.

## What your audience gets

- **Formats** — EPUB, PDF and MOBI are read in the app. The reader picks the best available format for each title automatically.
- **Reading settings** — a quick settings sheet lets the reader choose a reading theme, Serif or Sans typeface, font size, line height, margins, brightness, and paged or scrolling flow. A read-aloud toggle is also available.
- **Never lose your place** — as the reader moves through a title, their position is synced (debounced, queued when offline, and merged so the furthest point always wins). When they reopen the title on any device, they continue exactly where they left off.
- **Offline reading** — if a title was downloaded, the reader runs entirely offline and shows an offline banner; nothing requires a connection.

## Reading vs. download-only

The reader is governed by an **in-app reader** setting:

- **On** — titles open inside the app, and "Continue reading" tracks progress. This suits streaming and media libraries.
- **Off (library default)** — the app is download-first: a title is delivered as a file to read in the device's own way, with no in-app reader or progress tracking.

You choose this per tenant from your settings, so the same app can be a full reading experience for one library and a simple download archive for another.

## What you control

- Turn the **in-app reader** on or off for your tenant.
- Reading preferences are **device-local** for each reader and never sync — they personalize without you managing them.
