# Profile & security

Each reader has an account hub in the Atlas App where they manage how they sign in, secure the app, and personalize the experience.

## What your audience gets

- **Account hub** — a single place linking to membership, devices, security, language, appearance, notifications and more.
- **Biometric lock** — readers can require Face ID, Touch ID or device biometrics to open the app. The toggle only appears when both the tenant allows it **and** the device has biometric hardware enrolled; otherwise an explanatory card is shown instead of a dead switch.
  - Turning it on first asks for a biometric check (proving the reader can pass the gate before locking themselves in).
  - The lock is **device-only** and never syncs between devices — it's a local protection, not an account setting.
- **Language** — readers pick the interface language from your tenant's enabled languages, or "Automatic" to follow the device. The app re-translates immediately.
- **Appearance** — Light, Dark, or "Automatic" to follow your brand theme. The app re-themes immediately.

## What you control

- **Biometric** — a per-tenant feature flag enables or disables the biometric lock option for your audience.
- **Languages** — the set of languages a reader can choose comes from your tenant configuration.
- **Theme** — your brand theme is the default appearance; readers can override Light/Dark locally.

Sensitive sign-in tokens are kept in the device's secure storage and are never mixed with personalization preferences, which are stored separately and stay on the device.
