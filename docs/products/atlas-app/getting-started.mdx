# Getting started

The Atlas App turns one shared codebase into your own branded mobile app. Each tenant gets a dedicated build with its own name, icon and identity, while everything visual and behavioural beyond that is resolved at runtime from your tenant configuration — so a single app can re-theme itself to be *yours*.

## What you provide

To stand up a branded build, you supply a small brand profile (a `brand.json` for your tenant):

- **App name** — the title shown on the home screen and in the store.
- **URL scheme & bundle / package id** — the app's unique identity on iOS and Android (for example, a reverse-domain id derived from your website). This cannot change over the air; changing it means a new build.
- **Icon & splash screen** — your launch artwork and splash background color.
- **Fallback theme & feature flags** — a safety net used only on the very first launch if your live configuration is briefly unreachable.

## What is fixed vs. what you can change later

| Set at build time | Changed anytime (no new build) |
|---|---|
| App name, icon, splash, bundle id, URL scheme | Colors and theme, copy, supported languages |
| Which platforms (iOS / Android / TV) | Feature flags (downloads, biometric, push, audio, video, manga) |
| Inclusion in a build channel | Store links, catalog, plans, branding |

Native identity is locked into the build; everything else lives in your tenant configuration and updates instantly.

## The path to launch

1. **Brand profile** — your name, icon, splash, and ids are captured.
2. **Build channel** — your app is assigned its own release channel so updates target only your tenant.
3. **Store accounts** — the published app is owned by your store accounts (App Store and Google Play).
4. **Go live** — configure branding, languages and features in your tenant settings, then publish.
