# White-label & branding

The Atlas App is white-label by design: one shared codebase becomes many fully branded apps. Your app shows your name, your colors and your content — with no sign of a shared platform.

## Two layers of branding

**Build-time (fixed in the app):**

- App name
- URL scheme and bundle / package id
- Icon and splash screen
- A fallback theme and feature flags (used only if your live configuration is briefly unreachable on first launch)

These are the app's native identity and can't change without a new build.

**Runtime (changed anytime, no new build):**

- Theme — colors and look (for example an editorial or cinematic style)
- Copy and supported languages
- Feature flags — downloads, biometric, push, audio, video, manga, in-app reader, and the subscription gate
- Store links, catalog, plans and curated rails

When the app launches, it reads your tenant configuration and re-themes itself to match. The server configuration always wins; the build-time fallback only fills in during a first-launch offline moment.

## Feature flags at a glance

| Flag | Effect when on |
|---|---|
| Downloads | Offline downloads available |
| Biometric | Biometric app-lock option |
| Push | Push notifications |
| Audio / Video / Manga | The matching players/readers |
| In-app reader | Read inside the app with progress (off = download-first) |
| Subscription required | Non-members see the paywall |

## What you control

Everything except the native identity is yours to change from your tenant settings — instantly, without a release. Adding a brand-new tenant means a new brand profile plus its own build.
