# Playback

For media libraries, the Atlas App plays both video and audio with a native, resume-aware experience. Players load only when a reader opens them, so they never slow down app start.

## What your audience gets

- **Video** — adaptive streaming video that adjusts quality to the connection. Playback resumes from where the viewer stopped.
- **Audio** — audiobooks and podcasts play with cover artwork, title and author shown, and resume support so a listener picks up where they left off.
- **Seamless resume** — like the reader, playback position is remembered, so switching titles or devices continues from the right spot.
- **Offline media** — when a title has been downloaded, it plays from the device without a connection.

## What you control

Playback is governed by two per-tenant feature flags:

- **Audio** — enables the audio player for audiobooks and podcasts.
- **Video** — enables the video player for shows and films.

For a library archetype these are off by default; streaming, TV, education, podcast and event archetypes turn the relevant players on. You can override these in your tenant settings, so the same app can be reading-only for one tenant and a full media player for another.

The app only ever requests content meant for the mobile surface, and the players are matched to each title's kind automatically — books open in the reader, audio opens the audio player, and video opens the video player.
