# Authentication

The Atlas App uses **passwordless, magic-link sign-in** — there are no passwords to remember and no guest mode. Signing in is the only way into the app, which keeps every reader's library, progress and membership tied to their account.

## What your audience gets

1. **Enter email** — on the branded login screen, the reader types their email and taps "Send access link."
2. **Receive the link** — your tenant emails a sign-in link to that address.
3. **Open the app** — tapping the link opens a short web bridge page that redirects straight back into the app and completes sign-in.
4. **They're in** — the reader lands in the app, signed in, with their library restored.

The login screen is fully branded: it shows your app name, a fan of cover art, and your theme colors. The "check your email" confirmation lets the reader go back and try another address if needed.

## The web bridge

The sign-in link points to a page on your website that then hands off to the app. This bridge is what lets a single link work reliably across devices and email clients, and it keeps the actual sign-in inside the app. No special setup is needed beyond your tenant's website being reachable.

## What you control

- **Email sender & branding** — sign-in emails are sent under your tenant.
- **Access gate** — whether new sign-ins go straight into the app or first see a paywall is controlled by the subscription setting; see [Freemium](/docs/products/atlas-app/freemium).
- After sign-in, readers manage their own profile, security and devices.
