# Content browser

The Atlas App gives your audience several ways to find what to read, watch or listen to next — an editorial home, a full catalog, and search — all themed to your brand.

## What your audience gets

- **Editorial home** — a featured hero ("reading of the month"), genre chips, and a stack of curated rails such as **Continue**, **New releases**, and **App-exclusive**. Rails degrade gracefully: if one fails to load, the rest of the page still appears.
- **Catalog** — an explore grid with a kind filter (All / Books / Manga / Audio / Video), genre chips, and infinite scrolling. The grid stays aligned across phones and tablets.
- **Search** — a search box with trending suggestions when empty, type-ahead suggestions as the reader types, and a full results grid. "No results" and offline states are handled clearly.
- **Authors & genres** — tapping an author or genre opens a focused view of their titles, and chips throughout the app jump straight into a filtered catalog.
- **Item pages** — each title has a detail page (the "ficha") with cover, description, available formats, and actions to read, download or save.

## What you control

- **Curated rails** — the home rails are driven by your tenant configuration, so you decide what to feature and in what order.
- **Catalog & genres** — your published catalog and its genres power the browse and search experiences automatically.
- **Surface availability** — titles can be marked as available everywhere, **app-only**, or web-only. App-only titles carry an "Exclusive in the app" badge; web-only titles are hidden in the app. See [App-exclusive content](/docs/products/atlas-app/app-exclusive-content).
