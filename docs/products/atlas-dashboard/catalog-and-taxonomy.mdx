# Catalog & Taxonomy

The **Taxonomy** screen (`/admin/taxonomy`) controls how your catalog is grouped and browsed. These
settings power the genre, author, and publisher pages on your public storefront.

## Genres

A repeating table defines your genres. Each row has:

| Column | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Slug | The URL segment for the genre (e.g. `fantasy`) |
| Label | The display name shown to visitors |
| Intro HTML | Optional introductory text for the genre page |

The last row is always blank so you can add a new genre; rows left empty are ignored when you save.

## Introductions

Two rich-text areas add introductory copy to your listing pages:

- **Authors intro** — appears on the authors index page.
- **Publishers intro** — appears on the publishers index page.

These help visitors (and search engines) understand each section at a glance.

## Cross-links

The **Cross-links** section lets you connect related sections of your catalog — for example linking a
genre to a matching author grouping. It is edited as a structured list of source/target mappings.

## Saving

Click **Guardar taxonomía** (Save taxonomy) to apply. Changes resolve per host, so your storefront
reflects them without a redeploy.

The catalog title and the templates used for taxonomy page titles live under
[SEO Configuration](/docs/products/atlas-dashboard/seo-configuration). To see where these pages
appear publicly, read [Storefront Pages](/docs/products/atlas-dashboard/storefront-pages).
