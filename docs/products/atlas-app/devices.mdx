# Devices

Because sign-in is tied to each reader's account, the Atlas App gives them a clear view of every device where they're signed in — and the ability to revoke access.

## What your audience gets

- **Device list** — each connected device shows an icon, a label, and when it was last seen.
- **Current device badge** — the device the reader is currently using is marked "Current" and can't revoke itself.
- **Revoke access** — any other device can be revoked with a confirmation prompt. This is how a reader removes a lost, sold or old device from their account.
- **Clear states** — the screen handles loading, an empty list, errors with a retry, and an offline state, so readers always know what's happening.

## Why it matters for operators

Device management is part of keeping each reader's account — and your library access — under their control. Since all device data is scoped to the reader's account within your tenant, a reader only ever sees and manages their own devices, never anyone else's.

## What you control

This is a per-reader self-service feature; there's no per-tenant configuration needed. It works automatically once a reader is signed in. Combined with magic-link sign-in and the optional biometric lock, it gives your audience a complete, self-managed account security story.
