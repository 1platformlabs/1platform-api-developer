# Push notifications

Push notifications help you bring readers back — new releases, account updates, and more — while keeping each reader in control of what they receive.

## What your audience gets

- **Opt-in permission** — a master "Allow notifications" switch requests the device's push permission. Until it's granted, the per-type options read as inactive (no dead switches), with a hint pointing to device settings.
- **Per-type preferences** — once allowed, readers choose which kinds of notifications they want, grouped into sections such as **New releases** and **Your account**.
- **In-app badge** — a bell in the app bar reflects unread notifications received while the app is open, and a notifications feed lists them newest-first, marking unread items.

When a reader enables notifications, the app registers their device so you can reach it; disabling notifications de-registers it. Each device is registered independently, and the same device can be registered safely more than once.

## What you control

- **Push** — a per-tenant feature flag turns push notifications on or off for your audience.
- **What you send** — new-release and account-related messages are sent under your tenant.

The reader's device identity used for push is kept separate from their sign-in credentials, so notifications never touch their secure account tokens.

> Note: push delivery requires a real installed build; it is not available in development preview tools.
